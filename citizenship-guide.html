Requirements

  • Be at least 18 years old
  • Willing to serve in military if called upon
  • Be living in state you are applying for citizenship for at least 3 months
  • No trips of 6+ months during 5 year period

    • Step 1: Receive a Green Card

    Once you entered the United States, expect to receive a resident card or green card which can take up to 90 days.


    Step 2: Waiting

    It is a lengthy process but you will finally be able to apply for citizenship 5 years after obtaining your green card. You must have resided in the United States for at least 30 months of the 5 years.

    Application for Naturalization

    File an applicaiton for citizenship (Form N-400) to USCIS and submit it before the 5 year period ends.

    Fingerprinting

    One month after the form is received, UCIS will take your fingerprints for a background check


    Step 3: Citizenship Exam

    Interview for Citizenship

    Before the actual citizenship test, there is an interview for citizenship (around 14 months since Form N-400 was first submitted), where USCIS verifies if all information collected is accurate

    Actual exam

    Exam split into two sections

  • English Language Test
  • Civics Test

    • Step 4: Oath of Allegiance

    After the citizenship interview, you will be mailed details about an Oath of Allegiance ceremony

    After the ceremony concludes, you will become officially an United States citizen with a Ceritifcate of Naturalization now under your belt!
